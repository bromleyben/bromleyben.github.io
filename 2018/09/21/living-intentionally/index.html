living-intentionally

Lately I’ve been thinking a lot about my attitude towards life. I’m beginning to notice that allow far to much to
simply… happen. I just allow myself to be present in situations where others make decisions around me
to which I can follow, but rarely do I ever initiate anything. Passivity, that’s what it is, I have been
so very passive for a very long time, probably my whole life. It may just be part of my nature, but I can
see the damage of neglect that my passivity causes. I am afraid that I will let my life slip into pathways
that I don’t really want to be on, just because I never made a real push into anything, I just let life happen.
Have I believed that this is the way God wants me to live? That this is somehow trusting his course? Maybe it is,
but it’s on a scale that goes deeper than just allowing the shape of my life to be molded by God. Even in the
everyday I almost never raise my hand to suggest what we will do, where we will go. I just ride the flow, whether
I like it or not. Whilst I feel tired from working, studying and not sleeping enough, I feel even more restless to
move beyond these excuses for inaction.

I want to start striving towards goals, to see good plans take shape in real form. Enough idle dreaming, I want
these dreams of mine to materialize and nourish my soul and the souls of those around me. I want to be used by God
to achieve things of purpose, to touch lives and make impact. My inner life has felt like greyscale, with the barest
flecks of color that I try to sprinkle on from meaningless sources. I want to be filled with the vibrancy of real living, taking in the fruits of truth and beauty to grow into a man of rich character, not merely a satisfactory one.
This will mean dropping off a lot of mindsets and habits I have been developing my entire life, so be it.
