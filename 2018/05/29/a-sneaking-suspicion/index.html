A sneaking suspicion

I have a sneaking suspicion that I am made for more than gears and wheels.

For years I have dedicated much of my mind
and being to the developing of “real” skills, those that will set me up for the future. Why did I go into electrical
engineering? Did I really have a deep interest in electronics? More likely I found the image of a brilliantly savvy
hacker or robotics engineer somewhat fantastical. I suspect I translated the appearance of an exciting career
building robots or bringing down government mainframes into the very real decision to study electrical engineering.
Needless to say, it was not quite what I pictured.

I’m writing this now because I have seen the neglect of my soul and spirit. Working every day with numbers, code and
machines has drained me of expression. A few years ago this would not have bothered me; I was still riding the hope
that my work and studies would take on the romanticized ease and coolness that fills movies and video games. Even now
that I have a good job, working with friends in a comfortable environment, I can tell that I need to do more to
cultivate deeper meaning and expression in my life. I feel dry and worn, like my heart has lost the oil that keeps it
running smoothly and it has begun grating on me. I hope this can be the start of oiling my heart, and refreshing my
soul.

In saying all of this, I am glad for my journey. I have learnt a lot about myself, even if I have lived in denial for
some time. The skills I have acquired are also valuable, at least some of them are. I genuinely enjoy web
development, particularly the front end side of things. I think the sense of behind-the-scenes interaction with
people is fun. I’m creating a stage for them to explore, helping them with whatever service they are needing. Ah,
but anyways. I am glad for these skills, but I think it’s time to aim for more than a set of abilities. I want to
become someone more, to be an individual that shapes the lives of the people around them. I don’t want to just be
fixing up code or contributing to some commercial venture, I want to explore and analyze the deeper things of life.
Too long have I been giving God a side column in my life, with my need to prove myself acting as the hero.

Lets see what happens.
