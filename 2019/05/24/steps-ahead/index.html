Steps Ahead

After high school, after uni, with the start of every new year there comes a time to plan the steps ahead. I am in one such season now, I have been for a while. I finished the last of my uni several months ago now, and graduated two weeks ago. The driving force of formal education is gone, a wide valley of choice opens before me, vast and indimidating.

It feels like I have been travelling upon a trail through the woods, a trail that until this point has been dotted with signs and waypoints. But I suddenly find myself stepping out into the open air, standing at the edge of a clearing that ranges beyond the edge of sight. There are mountains on the horizon, though I know not how to get there.

I think I have been camping at the edge of that wood for the past few months. I don’t think this is such a bad thing, but I know sooner or later I will need to leave the comfort of the shade to seek out more furtile pastures. I suppose it is something like a quarter-life crisis, a transition of great significance. I am thinking a lot about what my next steps should be, what my aims should be and how I can expect to meet them.

Being a pretty passive person this is incredibly hard, I find it all to easy to just be flexible and “go with the flow”. Now that I have stepped out onto the bank, I have to choose which way to walk.

A number of key figures come to mind when considering this future, in no particular order they resemble:

  • Figuring out what work I am going to do longer term.
  • Finding my role in the church, or within missions.
  • Learning how best to lead and love my little family.
  • Deciding what skills or knowledge I am going to pursue now that my studies are complete.
  • Seeking out relationships that I can sow into and be nourished from.

Exploring these things are my chief goal for the next few months. Lets see how it goes.
